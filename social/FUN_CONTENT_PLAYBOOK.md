# Fun Content Playbook — Relocation Manager

## Tone

Funny, blue-collar, practical, and a little spicy — but not cruel.

The joke is usually:

> “This load went sideways because the details/trust/verification were wrong. Should’ve gone with Relocation Manager.”

## Safety rules

- Do not make fun of injuries, deaths, disasters, or people losing their livelihood.
- Do not use random copyrighted crash videos unless we have permission or a valid licensed source.
- Prefer licensed stock clips, user-submitted clips with written permission, or original/generated content.
- Blur license plates, faces, company names, DOT numbers, and addresses unless the owner gave permission.
- Keep jokes aimed at bad process, vague load details, scammy brokers/customers, and poor planning — not at drivers trying to work.

## Recurring formats

### 1. “Should’ve gone with Relocation Manager”

Use when a load clearly needed better verification or equipment matching.

Examples:

- Box truck shows up but freight needed a liftgate.
- Customer says “easy load” and it is a 9,000 lb machine.
- Broker changes pickup address after booking.
- Driver arrives and nobody has a forklift.

Caption formula:

> When they said “small box truck load” but forgot to mention it needed a forklift, a liftgate, and three prayers.  
> Should’ve gone with Relocation Manager.

### 2. “POV: You accepted the load without details”

Good for reels.

Script:

- Clip: truck chaos / confused driver / funny stock footage
- Text overlay 1: “POV: the post said ‘easy box truck load’”
- Text overlay 2: “But nobody mentioned the liftgate…”
- End card: “Verify the load before you roll. Relocation Manager.”

### 3. “Red flag load posts”

Short educational/funny series.

Examples:

- “Pays after customer sends check”
- “Must unload by hand — 8,000 lbs”
- “No pictures, no dimensions, no broker info”
- “Text this other number after booking”

CTA:

> We’re building a safer load board for small carriers.

### 4. “Equipment mismatch of the week”

Funny but educational.

Example:

> A 15 ft box truck, a 26 ft liftgate, and a pickup + trailer are NOT the same thing.

CTA:

> Relocation Manager matches by real equipment, not vague categories.

## Search/source ideas

Use licensed/free stock sources first:

- Pexels: trucking, delivery truck, warehouse, logistics, forklift, traffic, box truck
- Pixabay: trucking videos, logistics videos, delivery videos
- Unsplash: truck logistics, warehouse, freight, delivery truck photos

For viral-style clips:

- Ask truckers to DM clips with permission.
- Add a simple permission line: “By submitting, you confirm you own the clip and allow Relocation Manager to repost/edit it with credit.”
- Avoid injuries, active accidents, plate visibility, or identifiable businesses unless permission is clear.

## Example reel scripts

### Reel 1 — Liftgate surprise

Text overlay:

1. “Broker: easy box truck load”
2. “The load: 4 pallets, no dock, no forklift”
3. “Driver: so who’s unloading this?”
4. “Should’ve gone with Relocation Manager.”

Caption:

Load details matter. Liftgate, ramp, dock, forklift, weight, dimensions — all of it.

Relocation Manager is being built to verify the details before drivers waste time rolling.

### Reel 2 — Truck + trailer belongs too

Text overlay:

1. “Small carrier doesn’t always mean box truck.”
2. “Some folks run truck + trailer and still need work.”
3. “Match the equipment. Match the lane. Get the job.”

Caption:

Truck + trailer operators belong in the small carrier conversation too.

### Reel 3 — Red flag broker

Text overlay:

1. “Red flag: ‘Text my cousin after booking’”
2. “Red flag: payment terms changed mid-load”
3. “Red flag: customer info doesn’t match the account”
4. “Trust goes both ways.”

Caption:

We’re building broker/payment trust and customer ratings into the process from day one.

### Reel 4 — Saved lane alerts

Text overlay:

1. “Stop refreshing load boards all day.”
2. “Save your lane.”
3. “Set your minimum rate.”
4. “Get alerted when verified work matches.”

Caption:

Saved lane alerts are one of the biggest ways Relocation Manager can help drivers get more jobs.
