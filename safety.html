Verify the carrier before the pickup

A subscription, profile badge, email, or text message is never enough by itself. Match official records and contact information before releasing freight.

1

FMCSA Company Snapshot

Search the USDOT number and match the legal name, address, operating status, phone, and authority.

2

Licensing & insurance

Review federal licensing and insurance records. Independently contact the insurer if the certificate or contact details do not match.

3

Insurance checklist

Check the insured name, coverage, cargo limits, exclusions, expiration, and issuing contact.

4

Freight fraud guide

Learn the warning signs for stolen identity, changed payment instructions, double brokering, and off-platform pressure.

Pickup checklist

Before the truck arrives

  1. Confirm the commodity, dimensions, weight, value, and handling requirements.
  2. Set a specific pickup window and named contact.
  3. Confirm dock, forklift, liftgate, ramp, pallet jack, and driver-assist needs.
  4. Match the driver, truck, plate, carrier name, USDOT/MC, and insurance.
  5. Photograph count and condition before loading.
  6. Keep rate confirmation, messages, bill of lading, pickup proof, and delivery proof.
