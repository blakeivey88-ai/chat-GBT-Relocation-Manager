Public safety center

Verify the company before freight changes hands

Use official records, confirm insurance directly, and keep the pickup details inside the platform. A paid subscription does not replace independent verification.

1

Look up USDOT / MC

Open the official FMCSA SAFER Company Snapshot and match the legal name, address, authority, and phone.

2

Check licensing & insurance

Review the federal licensing and insurance record. Contact the insurer independently when anything looks inconsistent.

3

Follow the insurance checklist

Check insured name, policy dates, cargo limits, exclusions, and certificate contact details before pickup.

4

Watch for fraud signals

Slow down for changed payment instructions, mismatched contacts, off-platform pressure, or authority details that do not line up.

Shipper pickup checklist

Before the truck arrives

  1. Confirm commodity, dimensions, weight, value, and special handling.
  2. Set a specific pickup window and a named site contact.
  3. Confirm dock, forklift, liftgate, ramp, pallet jack, and driver-assist needs.
  4. Match the truck, plate, driver identity, carrier name, USDOT/MC, and insurance.
  5. Photograph freight condition and count before loading.
  6. Keep rate confirmation, messages, bill of lading, pickup proof, and delivery proof together.
Driver pain → product checks

We turn trucking headaches into load checks.

The goal is not just more posts. It is fewer bad-fit, low-trust, unclear, stale loads wasting a driver’s day.

Ghost / duplicate loads

Require expiration, last-confirmed time, and duplicate detection.

Vague details

Ask for weight, dimensions, dock, forklift, liftgate/ramp, driver assist, commodity, and photos.

Wrong equipment category

Match exact equipment instead of throwing every driver into the same generic load bucket.

No response

Track response behavior and let drivers rate communication after jobs.

Payment / double-brokering risk

Use payment grade, identity checks, customer ratings, and off-platform-pressure flags.

Rates below target

Let drivers save lane targets and warn when a load is below their minimum.

Core verification

Verify the carrier before booking.

No booking until DOT/MC lookup, ID check, insurance upload, customer/broker verification, and manual admin approval are complete.

1

DOT/MC lookup

Check authority, business name, operating status, and dispatch identity before a load is bookable.

2

ID check

Match the driver or broker photo ID to the account, phone, and company contact before approval.

3

Insurance upload

Collect cargo/liability certificates, expiration dates, coverage amounts, and file uploads.

4

Customer / broker verification

Score payment history, disputes, quick-pay availability, and scam risk before the lane opens.

5

Manual admin approval

An admin signs off last. No booking until the review is marked approved.

6

Scam prevention

Flag stolen authority patterns, suspicious payment behavior, off-platform pressure, fake contacts, and repeated disputes.
